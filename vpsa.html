THE THESIS

Culture isn't something we have.

It's something we do.

and the frat feels best when we're doing it together, on the same timing.

🏕️
retreat
/rəˈtriːt/
verb.
"to go somewhere as a frat and come back tighter than we left."
The problem:
  • Last retreat = no agenda = everyone doing their own thing
What worked:
  • Squid Games. Whole org playing together. That's the blueprint.
The fix:
  • Every retreat gets a theme (Gambling Night, Among Us, Harry Potter - sorted into houses, inter-house competition)
  • Full agenda - games, competitions, meals, moshes, same clock
  • Everyone moves together. Nobody drifts.
  • Mosh closes every night - I got the decks.
mix
/mɪks/
verb.
"to put the frat in a room with people who aren't us and let chemistry do its thing."
  • 1 mixer per month, minimum - committed
  • Named targets: AKPsi, DSP, Theta Tau
  • Beyond parties - activity mixers, day events, pregame collabs
  • Start going to more festivals together - that's peak bonding
  • If our culture is worth having, it's worth exporting

P.S. a lot of us are going to Hard Summer this summer. let's run it.

  • The inter-chapter network is there, we just haven't used it. This changes that.
